1D. If a minus sign appears, it means the that the z-value (probability) is below, or to the left of, the mean. If the probability is negative, the sigma value is also a negative value.

2A. Chi-square distribution is the distribution of a sum of the squares of k number of degrees of freedom. This distribution has the one parameter k, which is the degrees of freedom, and it is often used in line-fitting.

If X_n are standard normal random variables, then (X_n)^2 has a Chi-squared distribution.

Chi-square distribution is a special case of Gamma distribution.

1D. A discrete probability and sigma value means that for each independent experiment there will be a discrete probability and sigma value. The probability and sigma of one experiment is not related to that of another experiment. This is because the integral of the probability density function takes the area under the curve in the shape of rectangles, which will be a discrete value.

1E. The mean does not have to be discrete because it's not like the median or the mode, where those numbers are discrete. Instead, it's the typical value across the entire distribution. The mean is the most typical value in a set of values, regardless if the values are discrete or continuous. The probabilities of successes in a binomial distribution are discrete, whereas the number of successes measured is continuous.
