1E. The mean does not have to be discrete because it's not like the median or the mode, where those numbers are discrete. Instead, it's the typical value across the entire distribution. The mean is the most typical value in a set of values, regardless if the values are discrete or continuous. The probabilities of successes in a binomial distribution are discrete, whereas the number of successes measured is continuous.
