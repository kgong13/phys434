  1. Show that the sum and average of an initially Gaussian distribution is also a Gaussian (can be analytic or numerical). How does the standard deviation of the resulting sum or average Guassian change? This is a hugely important result. Explore what this means for integrating a signal over time.

As the distribution is convolved more often, the standard deviation of the resulting sum/average Gaussian increases. The high standard deviation means that values are very spread out compared to the mean. That means for integrating a signal over time, the signal gets larger and wider. The more times a signal is integrated over, the greater the signal becomes.

def gaussian(x, mu, sig):
    return np.exp(-np.power(x - mu, 2.) / (2 * np.power(sig, 2.)))

x_values = np.linspace(-3, 3, 120)

# initial gaussian distribution
p = gaussian(x_values, 0, 1)
plt.plot(p)
print("Standard deviation of intial Gaussian distribution:", np.std(p))

# convolutions
p2 = np.convolve(p, p)
p3 = np.convolve(p2, p)
p4 = np.convolve(p3, p)
p5 = np.convolve(p4, p)

print("Standard deviation of summed/averaged over Gaussian distribution:", np.std(p5))

# plotting the convolutions
plt.plot(p2)
plt.plot(p3)
plt.plot(p4)
plt.plot(p5)

plt.show()
